la vida & epoca de leonardo da vinci

The Great Leonardo

Leonardo Da Vinci was one of history's greats; a true polymath with interests as diverse as art, mechanics, anatomy and the science of flight.

Read more about his unique insights that remain as relevent today as they were over 500 years ago

World famous artist

Without a doubt one of the most famous paintings in the world, The Mona Lisa was painted some time between 1503 and 1519. It is now housed permanently in the Louvre Museum, Paris.

Leonardo was well known as an artist in his day, but that was just one aspect of his life's work.

Fantastical Flying Machines

Leonardo was fascinated by the idea of human flight, and over his lifetime he sketched numerous machines with the aim of self-powered flight. In recent times, some of his less fanciful machines have been built and shown to be sound from an engineering perspective.

Read more about Da Vinci's capacity for understanding science and nature.

Anatomy and Nature

Da Vinci harboured a great desire to discover what made nature work, and his work with the anatomy of the human body, along with disections of animals and plant studies, demonstrate an amazing degree of understanding. The most famous result of this interest is the Vitruvian Man.

Read more about Cartography.

Mapping his world

As well as the fascination with machines and nature, Leonardo wanted to document and understand the topography of his world. He developed an extensive set of maps, demonstrating real skill as a cartographer.

Read more about Leonardo's inventions.

Incredible Inventions

With such a diverse set of skills, it's little wonder that Leonardo Da Vinci was responsible for some amazing inventions including instruments of war, water displacement devices and of course his flying machines.
